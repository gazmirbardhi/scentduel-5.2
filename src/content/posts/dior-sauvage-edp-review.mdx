## The Best-Seller

Dior Sauvage Eau de Parfum launched in 2018 as the richer, deeper follow-up to the 2015 Eau de Toilette. François Demachy, Dior's then-house perfumer, took the bergamot-pepper-ambroxan skeleton of the EDT and amplified the lavender, added a nutty, sweet-Sichuan-pepper heart, and deepened the ambroxan base. The result is the best-selling men's fragrance in the world — a title it has held since roughly 2019.

Sauvage is also the most polarizing fragrance on the internet. Enthusiasts call it generic, synthetic, and overplayed. The buying public has made it the default men's fragrance of the late 2010s and early 2020s. Both things are true. This review tries to separate the scent from the discourse.

## Scent Profile

The opening is a huge hit of bergamot — sharp, citrus, and slightly peppery, with a calibrated dose of Sichuan pepper behind it. Within five minutes the heart emerges: lavender, geranium, and a sweet, almost nutty ambroxan that is the signature of the entire Sauvage line. By the one-hour mark the composition settles into its long dry-down: ambroxan, patchouli, and a clean white musk, with the lavender still faintly present.

The defining material is **ambroxan** — a synthetic ambergris-replacement that gives Sauvage its enormous projection and its slightly salty, mineral sweetness. Ambroxan is also the reason Sauvage is divisive: it is a large molecule that some noses smell as "clean and musky" and others smell as "scratchy and synthetic." If you are in the second camp, no amount of reassurance will change your mind.

## Performance

- **Longevity:** 8–10 hours on skin, with the ambroxan base detectable at 12+ hours on fabric. Sauvage is a longevity monster.
- **Sillage:** Very strong for the first 2 hours, strong for the next 2, then moderate. This is a fragrance that announces you — which is either the point or the problem, depending on the setting.
- **Versatility:** Good, leaning date-night and evening. Sauvage is wearable to the office at 2 sprays, but its default setting is a date, a night out, or any situation where you want to be noticed.

## Value

At $135 / 100ml retail (and $100–110 through discounters), Sauvage is the same price tier as Bleu de Chanel. For the performance, it is good value — you will rarely need to reapply, and a single bottle lasts most wearers a year or more.

The budget alternative is **Armaf Blue Homme** ($25–30 / 100ml), which is a closer Sauvage smellalike than the more commonly cited Club de Nuit Intense Man (which is an Aventus alternative, not a Sauvage one).

## The Verdict

Dior Sauvage EDP is a well-made, high-performance fragrance that has earned its popularity even if it has also earned its backlash. It is not the most interesting or the most subtle fragrance, but it does exactly what it was designed to do: project, last, and get compliments. If you want a fragrance that guarantees a reaction, this is it. If you want a fragrance that rewards close attention, look elsewhere.

**Rating: 8 / 10**

- Scent: 7/10 — effective but not subtle
- Performance: 10/10 — best-in-class longevity and projection
- Versatility: 8/10 — strong but leans evening
- Value: 8/10 — fairly priced for the performance
- Uniqueness: 5/10 — the most-worn men's fragrance in the world

**Recommended for:** date night, projection, compliments. **Not recommended for:** the office at more than 2 sprays, or anyone who wants to smell unique.
